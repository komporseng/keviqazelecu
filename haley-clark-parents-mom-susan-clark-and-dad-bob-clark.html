Meet Susan Clark, Haley Clark Mom

Susan Clark, Haley Clark’s mom, was born on 30 November 1956. She is the daughter of Natalie and Alex McKinnon.

Susan was the Putnam City High School valedictorian. She was a Kappa Alpha Theta sorority member while she was a student at the University of Oklahoma. She received recognition as the RUF/NEK Homecoming Queen and had numerous jubilant rides on the Sooner Schooner during OU football games. After earning a degree in fashion illustration from her formal schooling, she pursued a job in graphic design at nearby advertising businesses.

After having children, Susan became their full-time mother, a job she relished and at which she excelled. Susan was a mentor, advocate, and cheerleader for the family.

Sadly, Susan passed away on 31 July 2016. Before her death, she was looking forward to her role in helping plan her other daughter Courtney’s upcoming wedding to her fiance, Eric Merriman, of Dallas, Texas on October 1, 2016.

Meet Bob Clark, Haley Clark Dad

Robert “Bob” Clark Jr. is the father of Haley Clark. He is the president of RW Clark Inc. since 1988, the first commercial python breeder in the world that supplies zoos, institutes, and private collectors.

Because his job is a little unconventional, people often inquire what piqued his interest in breeding reptiles. He answers, “I didn’t get that way, I WAS that way!” I really don’t remember a time when I wasn’t trying to catch, keep or breed some kind of reptile. My parents were supportive but, that said, we had the “no snakes rule” at our house. I kept mostly turtles in those days. My grandfather would bring me box turtles he found crossing the road. I’d keep them until they escaped the flimsy outdoor enclosures I’d constructed for them.”

Bob kept pursuing his interest through high school and college, eventually getting a BS in Biology from the University of Kansas and a Master’s Degree in Herpetology there as well. He worked full-time in a clothing store till 1988.

He later partnered with reptile dealer Tom Crutchfield and left his job in a clothing store in 1988 to become a full-time breeder.

According to his website, he breeds ball pythons, blood pythons, Burmese pythons, reticulated pythons, and many more.

Bob was born on 3 July 1955, so he is currently 67 years old.
