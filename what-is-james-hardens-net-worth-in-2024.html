James Harden, a prominent name in the NBA, has not only made a mark in the basketball world with his exceptional skills but has also amassed a significant net worth through his lucrative NBA career, endorsements, and business ventures. In 2024, his estimated net worth stands at an impressive $165 million, making him one of the highest-paid athletes globally.

NBA Career and Earnings

Harden's journey to stardom began when he was selected as the third overall pick by the Oklahoma City Thunder in the 2009 NBA Draft, signing a two-year rookie deal worth $4.76 million. He then went on to achieve enormous success in the league, earning over $300 million in NBA salaries over his 14 seasons in the league. Throughout his career, Harden has been a 10-time All-Star, seven-time All-NBA Team selection, Sixth Man of the Year, three-time NBA scoring champion, two-time NBA assists leader, and NBA MVP.

After overachieving in the 2011-12 season, Harden left the Thunder franchise to sign with the Houston Rockets on a five-year contract worth around $80 million. His exceptional performance and offensive dominance led to him being elevated to a franchise player, and he continued to excel, ultimately signing a four-year deal worth $228 million with the Rockets. Harden's significant contributions to the Rockets' success on the court, combined with his high-scoring performances, solidified his position as one of the elite players in the NBA.

Trades and Contracts

Harden's career took an interesting turn when he played for the Philadelphia 76ers in the 2022-23 season and then joined the Los Angeles Clippers in 2023 after a trade deal. He was reported to have signed a two-year $68 million contract with the Philadelphia 76ers, with a salary of $33 million for the 2022-23 season and $35 million for the 2023-24 season. Despite opting to remain with the 76ers by picking up his $35.6 million option, he eventually requested a trade, leading to his move to the Clippers in October 2023.

Endorsements and Business Ventures

Apart from his NBA earnings, Harden's net worth is significantly boosted by his endorsements and business ventures. He has partnered with major brands such as Adidas, with whom he signed a 13-year deal worth $200 million, earning approximately $22 million annually from endorsements alone. Harden's endorsement deals extend to various brands such as Therabody, Pura, BodyArmor, Vanguart, Gopuff, Block, Inc, and more, showcasing his wide-reaching influence and marketability.
