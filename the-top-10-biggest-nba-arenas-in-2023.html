The Top 10 Biggest NBA Arenas in 2023

August 15, 2024
blog

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is home to some of the most iconic and largest arenas in the world. These arenas not only serve as venues for thrilling basketball games but also provide an unforgettable experience for fans. In this article, we will explore the top 10 biggest NBA arenas in 2023, based on their seating capacity.

1. United Center - Chicago Bulls

With a seating capacity of 20,917, the United Center takes the top spot as the biggest NBA arena. Located in Chicago, Illinois, it is the home of the Chicago Bulls. The arena boasts state-of-the-art facilities, creating an electric atmosphere for spectators.

2. Madison Square Garden - New York Knicks

Madison Square Garden, located in New York City, is not only one of the most famous arenas in the NBA but also the second-largest with a seating capacity of 19,812. Being the home of the New York Knicks, it has a rich history and is known for its passionate fans.

3. Scotiabank Arena - Toronto Raptors

Ranking third on the list, the Scotiabank Arena in Toronto, Canada, has a seating capacity of 19,800. It is home to the Toronto Raptors and has hosted various significant NBA events, including the NBA Finals in 2019.

4. Wells Fargo Center - Philadelphia 76ers 

The Wells Fargo Center, with a seating capacity of 19,500, is the fourth-largest NBA arena. Situated in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, it serves as the home of the Philadelphia 76ers. The arena offers excellent amenities and a vibrant atmosphere for fans.

5. Staples Center - Los Angeles Lakers & Los Angeles Clippers

Located in Los Angeles, California, the Staples Center has a seating capacity of 19,068, making it the fifth-largest NBA arena. It is the shared home of the Los Angeles Lakers and the Los Angeles Clippers. The arena has witnessed numerous iconic NBA moments.

6. American Airlines Center - Dallas Mavericks

With a seating capacity of 19,200, the American Airlines Center ranks sixth in terms of size. It is the home of the Dallas Mavericks and is located in Dallas, Texas. The arena provides a comfortable and enjoyable experience for basketball enthusiasts.

7. Smoothie King Center - New Orleans Pelicans

The Smoothie King Center, situated in New Orleans, Louisiana, has a seating capacity of 18,500, securing the seventh spot on our list. It serves as the home of the New Orleans Pelicans and offers a unique and vibrant basketball atmosphere.

8. State Farm Arena - Atlanta Hawks

Ranking eighth, the State Farm Arena in Atlanta, Georgia, has a seating capacity of 17,600. It is home to the Atlanta Hawks and is known for its modern design and excellent sightlines, ensuring an immersive experience for fans.

9. FedExForum - Memphis Grizzlies

With a seating capacity of 17,381, the FedExForum in Memphis, Tennessee, takes the ninth spot on our list. It serves as the home of the Memphis Grizzlies and has a reputation for being a fan-friendly arena, offering various amenities.

10. Spectrum Center - Charlotte Hornets

Rounding out the top 10 is the Spectrum Center in Charlotte, North Carolina, with a seating capacity of 17,265. It is the home of the Charlotte Hornets and is known for its passionate fan base and lively atmosphere.

The NBA is home to some of the largest and most iconic arenas in the world, providing basketball fans with unforgettable experiences. From the United Center in Chicago to Madison Square Garden in New York City, these arenas not only host thrilling games but also offer state-of-the-art facilities and vibrant atmospheres. Whether you're a fan of the Bulls, Knicks, or any other NBA team, attending a game in one of these top 10 biggest NBA arenas is sure to leave you with lasting memories.
